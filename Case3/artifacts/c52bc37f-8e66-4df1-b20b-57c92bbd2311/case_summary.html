AGSI Case Study 2 · Spatial Reasoning Fidelity

Proximity-Based Spatial Modeling of Hazardous-Waste Exposure and Asthma Prevalence (North Carolina)

An Autonomous Geospatial Scientific Inquiry (AGSI) framework demonstration.

Research Question

How does proximity to hazardous waste facilities influence spatial patterns of adult asthma prevalence across census tracts of North Carolina, and how do these relationships vary spatially by socioeconomic and demographic context?

What This Case Demonstrates

This case evaluates the framework's spatial reasoning fidelity — its ability to translate a research question whose central verb is geometric ("proximity to") into a coherent sequence of geometry-aware operations without being told how. Unlike Case 1, the four required datasets are user-supplied; the burden shifts onto the system's capacity to engineer derived spatial features, make sound geographic decisions (CRS, tract representation, contiguity), and assemble a coherent ladder of spatial inference.

Geometric feature engineering CRS-aware analysis Local cluster detection Spatially varying regression

Workflow Summary

1. RQ Understanding

Identified asthma prevalence as the response, hazardous-waste proximity as the principal exposure, and tract socioeconomic composition as moderating context. Confirmed the supplied registry as sufficient.

2. Planning

Generated a 2-objective plan: (i) test spatial clustering and proximity alignment with high-asthma hotspots; (ii) estimate adjusted, contextually moderated, and spatially varying associations.

3. Execution

Reprojected to EPSG:32119 (NC State Plane, meters); derived nearest-facility distance and 5 km facility counts from tract centroids; applied Global Moran's I, Getis–Ord Gi*, Mann–Whitney U, OLS with HC1 errors and interactions, and GWR with adaptive bandwidth.

4. Result Analysis & Reporting

Result Analysis Agent compiled hotspot maps, proximity comparisons, and per-coefficient GWR thematic maps; Reporting Agent assembled the final manuscript end-to-end.

Data Sources (User-Supplied)
